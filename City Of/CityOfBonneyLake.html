City of Bonney Lake

This construction project involves the installation of 1,400 linear feet (LF) of curb, gutter, and a 5-foot wide sidewalk along various streets within the City of Bonney Lake, specifically within the City's right-of-way (ROW). Additionally, the project includes the implementation of a stormwater collection system and utility modifications that are necessary to accommodate the newly constructed sidewalks. The contractor is responsible for completing these tasks in a specific order, with the first phase focusing on Angeline Rd E from Van Dyk St to 106th St.

  • Scope of Work: The primary objective of this project is to improve the infrastructure and pedestrian amenities within the City of Bonney Lake. This includes the installation of curb, gutter, and a 5-foot wide sidewalk along various streets. The new sidewalks will enhance pedestrian safety, accessibility, and overall usability.
  • Location: The project covers multiple streets within the City of Bonney Lake. However, the initial phase concentrates on Angeline Rd E, spanning from Van Dyk St to 106th St. This area has been chosen as the starting point for construction.
  • Curb and Gutter: The project includes the construction of curbs and gutters. Curbs provide a boundary between the sidewalk and the road, helping to channelize stormwater and prevent erosion. Gutters collect and direct rainwater toward stormwater drains.
  • Sidewalk: A 5-foot wide sidewalk is to be constructed along these streets. Sidewalks provide safe pedestrian pathways, which are essential for the community's convenience and safety.
  • Stormwater Collection System: To manage rainwater runoff effectively, a stormwater collection system will be integrated into the project. This system will help mitigate flooding and erosion issues and protect the environment by preventing pollutants from entering waterways.
  • Utility Modifications: Utility modifications are required to accommodate the new sidewalks. This might involve adjusting or relocating existing utilities such as water lines, sewer lines, gas lines, or electrical infrastructure. These adjustments ensure that the utilities remain accessible and functional.
  • Construction Order: The project's construction tasks are to be completed in a specific order, with the first focus being on Angeline Rd E from Van Dyk St to 106th St. This phased approach allows for efficient progress and minimizes disruptions to the entire city at once.
  • Overall Benefits: Upon completion, this project will enhance the city's infrastructure, making it more accessible and safer for pedestrians. It will also address stormwater management concerns and ensure that utilities remain functional while coexisting with the new sidewalks.
  • Community Impact: During construction, there may be temporary inconveniences such as road closures, detours, and construction noise. However, the long-term benefits of improved infrastructure and safety will greatly outweigh these inconveniences, contributing to the overall quality of life in Bonney Lake.

Summary

In summary, this construction project in the City of Bonney Lake involves significant upgrades to the city's streets and sidewalks, prioritizing safety, accessibility, and stormwater management. The phased approach ensures that the work progresses efficiently while minimizing disruptions to the community.
