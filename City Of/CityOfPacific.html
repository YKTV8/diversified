City of Pacific

  1. Asbestos Abatement in Flooring Mastic:The project begins with the critical task of asbestos abatement. Asbestos is a hazardous material, particularly when found in flooring mastic. Safely removing and disposing of asbestos is essential to protect the health of workers and building occupants.
  2. Selective Flooring Removal:Existing flooring materials, including vinyl, carpet, and tile, will be selectively removed at specific locations within the Senior Center. This removal is likely part of the overall aesthetic and functional improvements planned for the facility.
  3. New Vinyl Flooring Installation:Following the removal of existing flooring, new vinyl flooring will be installed in designated areas. This can enhance the appearance and functionality of the space, making it more appealing and easier to maintain.
  4. Restroom Remodeling for ADA Compliance:The project includes comprehensive remodeling of both restrooms within the Senior Center to ensure they comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) standards. ADA-compliant facilities are essential for accessibility and inclusivity.
  5. Replacement of Gymnasium Ceiling Tiles:The gymnasium ceiling tiles will be replaced. This can improve the overall appearance of the space and may also have acoustic benefits, making the gym more suitable for various activities and events.
  6. Patch and Repaint Walls:Designated walls within the Senior Center will be patched and repainted. This step can have a transformative effect on the overall aesthetics, creating a fresh and inviting atmosphere for center visitors.
  7. Installation of Audio-Visual System:An audio-visual system will be installed in the gymnasium. This can enhance the Senior Center's capabilities for presentations, events, and multimedia activities, providing a more versatile space for the community.
  8. Theatrical Drape System Installation:A theatrical drape system will be installed, likely in the gymnasium. This can provide the Senior Center with the flexibility to create different event settings, such as stages or partitions, as needed.
  9. Wall-Mounted Sport Protection Pads:Wall-mounted sport protection pads will be installed, ensuring safety during physical activities in the gymnasium. These pads can help prevent injuries during sports and recreational events.

Summary

In summary, this construction project involves a range of renovations and asbestos abatement at the Senior Center. It aims to enhance the facility's accessibility, aesthetics, functionality, and safety. These improvements will not only benefit the immediate users of the center but also contribute to the overall quality of community life and engagement at this vital community resource.
