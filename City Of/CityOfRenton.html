City of Renton

Current Discharge Situation: The current stormwater management system collects runoff in catch basins and pipes, directing it to the Heather Downs Detention Pond. However, this pond currently does not provide any treatment to the runoff. As a result, the collected stormwater flows directly into Maplewood Creek, which ultimately reaches Lake Washington. This unfiltered discharge can contribute to water pollution in these natural bodies of water.


  • Project Objective: The primary goal of this construction project is to retrofit the existing city-owned Heather Downs Detention Pond into a combined facility that serves both as a detention basin and a water quality treatment facility. This retrofitting is essential to address two key issues: Flow Control & Water Quality treatment.
  • Flow Control: By functioning as a detention basin, the facility will manage the flow of stormwater, preventing rapid and potentially damaging surges of water during heavy rainfall events. This helps mitigate flooding downstream.
  • Water Quality Treatment: The retrofit will introduce mechanisms for treating stormwater, removing pollutants, and improving its quality before it is discharged. This is vital for protecting the ecological health of Maplewood Creek, the Cedar River, and ultimately, Lake Washington.
  • Treatment Mechanisms: The construction project will likely include the installation of various treatment mechanisms within the detention pond. These may involve sedimentation basins, vegetated swales, filtration systems, or other technologies designed to capture and remove pollutants from the runoff.
  • Environmental Impact: The retrofit of the detention pond into a combined facility will have a positive environmental impact. It will help protect local water bodies, preserving their water quality and supporting the health of aquatic ecosystems. This, in turn, contributes to the overall environmental sustainability of the area.
  • Community Benefits: The project benefits the community by reducing the risk of flooding in the area, enhancing the quality of local water bodies, and potentially improving property values and quality of life for residents in the Heather Downs residential development.

This construction project aims to address stormwater runoff issues in a 30-acre area characterized by impervious surfaces. By retrofitting the Heather Downs Detention Pond into a combined detention and water quality treatment facility, it will provide both flow control and water quality improvement for the stormwater runoff, ultimately benefiting the environment and the local community by reducing flooding risks and protecting the health of nearby water bodies.
